# Chocolate-Lauch-Data-Analysis
## Project Objective
The objective of this project is to analyze customer survey data to optimize the market launch strategy for our new product, **Choconutties**. By examining demographic preferences, purchasing habits, and customer satisfaction metrics, this analysis provides data-driven recommendations regarding target audience segmentation, product variety focus, and pricing strategies to maximize market adoption.

## Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) & Business Questions

### Existing KPIs Analysed:
1. **Total Sample Size:** 51 respondents.
2. **Average Respondent Age:** ~30.5 years old.
3. **Top Preferred Chocolate Type:** Dark Chocolate (26 out of 51 preferences).
4. **Overall Satisfaction Rating:** 2.73 out of 4.
5. **Total Grocery Spend:** 10,810 units.
6. **Total Chocolate Bars Purchased:** 181 bars.

### Additional KPIs Added for Launch Strategy:
7. **Dark Chocolate Market Share:** 51% of total survey preference.
8. **Gender Preference Split (Dark Chocolate):** 61.5% of male respondents vs. 38.5% of female respondents prefer Dark.
9. **Bulk Purchase Likelihood Rate:** Percentage of consumers highly likely to purchase in bulk (Rating 3 or 4).
10. **Choco-Bar Consumption Velocity:** Average number of chocolate bars purchased per customer (~3.5 bars).

## Data Analysis Process
1. **Data Cleaning:** Processed the raw survey responses to ensure data integrity, formatting column headers (`Customer`, `Age`, `Sex`, `Groceries`, `Choco-bars`, `Type`, `Satisfied`, `Bulk`), and verifying data consistency across all 51 customer entries.
2. **Pivot Table Analysis:** Formulated structural summaries to segment data across multiple dimensions:
   * Chocolate Type Preference counts.
   * Customer Satisfaction Distribution.
   * Gender vs. Chocolate Type Cross-tabulations.
   * Bulk purchase intent metrics.
3. **Dashboard Engineering:** Synthesized pivot charts, KPI cards, and interactive slicers in Microsoft Excel to create a cohesive business intelligence tool for the product team.

---

## Key Insights

### 1. Chocolate Type Dominance
* **Dark Chocolate** is the absolute favorite, capturing **51%** of the entire consumer preference pool (26 votes out of 51).
* **White Chocolate** follows second with 15 votes, while **Milk Chocolate** sits at the bottom with 10 votes.

### 2. Demographic & Gender Segmentation
* **Males** have a heavy preference skew toward Dark Chocolate (16 out of 27 males chose Dark).
* **Females** show a more distributed preference across profiles but lean slightly toward White Chocolate (9 out of 24 females).

### 3. Purchasing Habits & Bulk Likelihood
* A significant cluster of buyers falls into the highest spending tiers on groceries (180–220 units), showing a strong positive correlation with high frequency chocolate bar consumption.
* The baseline metric indicates high volume potential if Choconutties introduces tiered packaging.

---

## Final Conclusion & Launch Recommendations
* **Primary Flavor Alignment:** Since **Choconutties** features a nut-based flavor profile, the initial product lineup should prioritize a **Dark Chocolate base variant**, as dark chocolate commands over half the addressable market segment.
* **Target Marketing:** Launch promotional campaigns tailored toward young adults (average age profile ~30) with gender-specific item bundles (e.g., highlighting dark chocolate attributes in male-focused demographics and creating premium white/milk chocolate nut variants for female segments).
* **Packaging Strategy:** Given the high grocery budget correlation, introduce **"Multi-packs" or "Bulk Pouches"** alongside single-bars to capitalize on high-volume household grocery buyers.
